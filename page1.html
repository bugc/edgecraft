Renew V-Shape - שחזור צורת קצה

The cutting edge has shape of V in cross section. When edge becomes worn we use steeling or honing to refresh edge's tip. Edge blunts again and meanwhile V turns to U. So it comes time to apply new grind. Steeling by rod was considered 'bad habit' and better to be replaced with stropping or honing.
Proper grind depends on purpose and personal habits. Most of cooks does not care what bevel and grind the knife was initially. They think new grind 'is sharp' when the knife is able to slice soft tomatoes. Same, the knife 'is good' if it takes much time (how?) to blunt edge to death. Such situation is too common to judge people.

When customer asks me to restore edge to be 'sharp like it was out of the box', straight V-grind will be applied at 15° or 20° per side - depends on knife brand and design. Two-step process with diamond abrasives and electric grinder are in use.

Apply modern grind - עיצוב שפוע מודרני

Old blades have tendency to thicken tips just because of wearing out or by old design. Thin edge convergence offers benefits of lower pressure needed to cut through (means lighter hand on repetative actions).
Novadays there is technology to convert legacy blades and give appropriate care for modern western knives. Regular V-shape could be upgraded with secondary bevel and uniform micro teeth pattern. With proper care most of old-style blades can withstand 15° primary bevel and thiner tip. Secondary bevel provides better resistancy to fold for steel used on older/cheaper western knives.

To provide lightweight cutting experience I recommend reshape edge to thiner convergence and apply secondary bevel. Micro teeth or ultra fine 3d bevel are optional as depend on everyday honing practice.

What ever more?- תיקון ועבדות שנות

Some knives need repair - refine curve of blade, some polishing or grip fix... To be polite not everything should be repaired but could.
 
There is no 'best' grind because new habits arise with each change. Same knife, same food processed but you have changed. The grind applied and honing practice both are involved in change. When you got familiar with new performance some tool for edge refreshening is reasonable needed. Actually I do not suggest strope or honing bar either. Better results are achived with coarse diamonds (400 grit) and ceramic rollers. 

Daily honing is really needed for professionals only, includes the need for periodical refreshing of grind's shape. Practically that means less efforts than first time. Overall technology is justified by time and price to provide high performance for years of blade life-cycle.
