Renew V-Shape - שחזור צורת קצה

The cutting edge has shape of V in cross section. When edge becomes worn we use steeling or honing to refresh edge's tip. But it blunts again and we repeat... V turns to U. So it comes to apply new grind. Steeling (by rod) was considered bad habit and to be replaced with stropping or honing (with abrasive paste).
The grind depends on purpose and personal habits in general. Most of cooks does not care what bevel and grind it was. They think new grind 'is sharp' when the knife is able to slice soft tomatoes. Same, the knife 'is good' if it takes much time (how?) to blunt edge to death. To be honest such situation is too common to judge people.
When customer asks to restore edge sharp like it was out of the box, the simpliest grind will be applied - 15° or 20° bevel per side. Two-step process with diamond abrasives and electric grinder are in use.

Apply modern grind - עיצוב שפוע מודרני

Old blades have tendency to thicken tips just because of wearing out or by old design. Novadays we have technology to convert legacy blades and give appropriate care for modern western knives.
Regular V-shape could be upgraded to two-bevel or even three-bevel when each side of edge arc is alike chisel - suitable for hard work combined with finest slice and cut ability.
Thin tip (edge convergence) offers benefits of lower pressure needed to cut through (thus lighter hand on repetative actions). With proper care most of old-style blades can withstand 15° bevels and\or thiner tip.
To provide lightweight cutting experience we suggest two improvements: grind edge to thiner convergence and change bevels to 25°-30° (in total).

What ever more?- תיקון ועבדות שנות

Some knives need repair: refine a line of blade, some polishing or grip fix... To be polite not everything should be repaired but could.
There is no 'best' grind because new habits arise with each change. Same knife, same food processed but you have changed. The grind applied and honing practice both are involved. When you got familiar with new performance some tool for edge refreshening is needed. Actually I do not suggest strope or honing bar either. Better results are achived with coarse diamonds (400 grit) and ceramic rollers. That means need of periodical refreshing of V-shape and restoring of fine second bevel on tip.
